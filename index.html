PROTEIN

What is protein?
Protein is essential to building and maintaining lean muscle mass! Proteins are usually found in animal products such as Chicken, Eggs and milk.
You should be aiming for 2.2g of protein per 1kg of bodyweight per day.

CARBS

What are carbohydrates?
Carbohydrates are commonly frowned upon, however they are vital for a healthy diet. Carbohydrates are sugars, starches and fibers found in various foods such as fruits, vegetables and breads.
You should be aiming for 4-7g per 1kg of bodyweight per day.

FATS

What are fats?
Although self explainatory and looked at in a bad way, fats are the primary function for body energy reserves. Fats also help absorb vitamins to keep your body healthy and functioning. Fat is in most foods however high-fat content foods that are healthy and not processed are easy to find! You can find fat in most dairy products, Beef and nuts.
You should be aiming for 0.5-2g of fat per 1kg of bodyweight per day.

CALORIC SURPLUS

What is a caloric surplus?
A caloric surplus is when the amount of calories that you consume is higher then the amount you burn based on your TDEE. In this state you gain body mass. You should enter a surplus when your body stops gaining mass and you are not progressing in the programs workout routine.
To enter a surplus you must consume 10% more of your TDEE calories.

CALORIC DIFECIT

What is a caloric difecit?
A caloric difecit is the exact opposite of a surplus, you must consume less calories based on your TDEE then your body requires. In this state you lose weight, but not muscle mass! You should enter a surplus when you feel like you are gaining unnecessary fat!
To enter a difecit you should consume 10% less then your TDEE calories
